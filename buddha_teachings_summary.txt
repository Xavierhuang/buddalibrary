Core Buddhist Concepts
----------------------

1. **The Three Marks of Existence**
   - Anicca (Impermanence): All things are transient and constantly changing
   - Dukkha (Suffering/Dissatisfaction): Life involves suffering or dissatisfaction
   - Anatta (Non-self): There is no permanent, unchanging self

2. **The Four Noble Truths**
   - The truth of suffering (Dukkha)
   - The truth of the cause of suffering (Samudaya - craving/attachment)
   - The truth of the end of suffering (Nirodha)
   - The truth of the path leading to the end of suffering (Magga)

3. **The Noble Eightfold Path**
   - Wisdom (Prajñā):
     1. Right Understanding
     2. Right Thought
   - Ethical Conduct (Śīla):
     3. Right Speech
     4. Right Action
     5. Right Livelihood
   - Mental Discipline (Samādhi):
     6. Right Effort
     7. Right Mindfulness
     8. Right Concentration

Key Buddhist Practices
---------------------

**Meditation**
- Samatha (calm abiding): Develops concentration and tranquility
- Vipassanā (insight): Develops wisdom and understanding of reality

**Mindfulness**
- Being fully present in the current moment
- Observing thoughts and feelings without judgment
- Cultivating awareness in daily activities

**Compassion (Karuna) and Loving-Kindness (Metta)**
- Developing genuine care for all beings
- Practicing the Four Immeasurables:
  1. Loving-kindness
  2. Compassion
  3. Sympathetic joy
  4. Equanimity

Important Buddhist Texts
------------------------

1. **Pali Canon (Tipitaka)**
   - Vinaya Pitaka: Monastic rules
   - Sutta Pitaka: Discourses of the Buddha
   - Abhidhamma Pitaka: Philosophical analysis

2. **Mahayana Sutras**
   - Heart Sutra (Prajñāpāramitā Hṛdaya)
   - Diamond Sutra (Vajracchedikā Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra)
   - Lotus Sutra (Saddharma Puṇḍarīka Sūtra)

3. **Other Important Texts**
   - Dhammapada: Collection of verses
   - Milinda Pañha: Dialogues between King Milinda and Nagasena
   - Visuddhimagga: Path of Purification by Buddhaghosa
